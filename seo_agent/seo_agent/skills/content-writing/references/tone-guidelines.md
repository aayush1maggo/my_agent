# Tone & Style Guidelines

## Default Writing Voice

**Brand voice:** Professional but approachable. Expert without being condescending.
**Perspective:** Second person ("you", "your") to speak directly to the reader.
**Register:** Plain English. Write at a Year 10 reading level unless the audience is technical.

---

## Core Style Rules

### Active voice over passive
- BAD: "The quote will be sent to you by our team."
- GOOD: "Our team will send you a quote."

### Concrete over vague
- BAD: "We provide high-quality services to meet your needs."
- GOOD: "We install ducted air conditioning in Melbourne homes within 3–5 days."

### Short sentences and paragraphs
- Maximum 20–25 words per sentence (most should be shorter)
- Maximum 4–5 lines per paragraph
- Use bullet points for lists of 3 or more items

### No filler phrases — cut immediately
These phrases add no value and signal AI-generated padding:
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "It's important to note that..."
- "When it comes to..."
- "In conclusion..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "Look no further than..."
- "In this comprehensive guide..."
- "Navigating the complexities of..."

### Numbers and specifics
- Use specific numbers: "over 500 customers" not "hundreds of customers"
- Use digits for numbers 10+ in body copy: "12 years experience" not "twelve years"
- Use percentages where possible: "reduces costs by up to 30%"

---

## Content Type Voice Adjustments

### Service pages
- Confident and reassuring
- Focus on outcomes ("you'll get X") not just features ("we offer X")
- Include trust signals: years in business, number of customers, certifications
- End every section with a micro-CTA or reinforcement of value

### Blog posts
- Helpful and educational
- Conversational but informative — like explaining to a smart friend
- Use "you" to address the reader directly
- OK to use rhetorical questions to break up sections

### Landing pages
- Urgent and outcome-focused
- Every sentence earns its place — no padding
- Emphasise what the reader gains, not what the company does
- CTA language: action verbs ("Get", "Book", "Start", "Claim") not passive ("Learn more")

---

## E-E-A-T Signals to Include

**Experience:** Reference real-world situations ("When we installed X for a customer in [suburb]...")
**Expertise:** Use correct industry terminology; explain why, not just what
**Authoritativeness:** Reference credentials, years in business, relevant associations
**Trustworthiness:** Include guarantees, clear pricing signals, transparent process

---

## What NOT to Write

- Do not make unverifiable claims ("the best in Australia")
- Do not use competitor names unless specifically instructed
- Do not promise specific results ("you will rank #1")
- Do not use ALL CAPS for emphasis — use bold or restructure the sentence
- Do not write click-bait titles ("You won't believe what...")
- Do not pad to hit word count — every paragraph must add information
